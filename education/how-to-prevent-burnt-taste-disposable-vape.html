Why Does My Disposable Vape Taste Burnt? Causes & Prevention

Published: 2026-07-20
Author: Vape 'R' Aus Education
Reading Time: 2 min read

There is nothing more frustrating than taking a puff from your favorite disposable vape only to experience a harsh, dry, and burnt taste in the back of your throat. Understanding why this happens and adopting proper usage habits can prevent this and prolong the life of your device.

How a Disposable Vape Works

Inside every disposable vape (like an IGET, Waka, or Alibarbar) is a battery, a heating coil, and a polyester fiber wick saturated with e-liquid. When you draw on the mouthpiece, the battery heats the coil, which vaporises the liquid held in the wick. If the wick is dry when the coil heats up, the cotton fibers burn, producing that characteristic burnt taste.

Key Causes of a Burnt Taste

  1. Chain Vaping (Rapid Puffs): Vaping continuously without pausing doesn't give the wick enough time to absorb more e-liquid. The cotton dries out locally around the coil and burns.
  2. Low E-Liquid Levels: If the device is close to its puff limit, the e-liquid is depleted. There is simply no liquid left to saturate the wick.
  3. Cold Ambient Temperatures: Cold weather increases the thickness (viscosity) of the e-liquid. Thicker e-liquid flows slowly, causing dry patches on the cotton wick.
  4. Drawing Too Hard: Inhaling with extreme force can pull liquid away from the coil area too quickly or cause uneven vaporisation.

How to Prevent a Burnt Taste

Follow these simple guidelines to make sure every drag is smooth and flavorful:

  • Pace Your Puffs: Wait at least 15 to 20 seconds between draws. This gives the capillary action of the wick time to pull e-liquid back onto the heating element.
  • Keep the Device Warm: Avoid leaving your vape in cold cars or unheated spaces during winter. If the device is cold, warm it up in your hands for a few minutes before puffing.
  • Store Upright: Keeping your vape vertical helps gravity distribute e-liquid evenly across the wick reservoir.
  • Know When to Dispose: If the burnt taste persists even after letting the device rest, the e-liquid reservoir is empty or the wick is permanently charred. Vaping a charred wick releases unpleasant smoke and should be avoided.
