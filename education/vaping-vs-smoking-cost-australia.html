Vaping vs. Smoking Cost in Australia: A Comprehensive Financial Breakdown

Published: 2026-07-18
Author: Vape 'R' Aus Education
Reading Time: 2 min read

With the cost of tobacco products rising steadily in Australia due to heavy taxation, many smokers are looking at the financial implications of their habit. The difference in cost between smoking traditional cigarettes and using disposable vapes is substantial. Here is a realistic, step-by-step financial breakdown of vaping vs. smoking costs in Australia.

The Cost of Traditional Smoking in Australia

As of 2026, a standard pack of 20 premium cigarettes in Australia costs between $35.00 and $45.00. For bulk buyers, a carton of 10 packs typically ranges from $320.00 to $400.00.

Let's look at the average cost for three types of smokers:

  • Light Smoker (5 cigarettes a day / ~2 packs a week): Approximately $80.00 per week, or $4,160.00 per year.
  • Moderate Smoker (10 cigarettes a day / ~3.5 packs a week): Approximately $140.00 per week, or $7,280.00 per year.
  • Heavy Smoker (20 cigarettes a day / 7 packs a week): Approximately $280.00 per week, or $14,560.00 per year.

For a heavy smoker, the cost of cigarettes is equivalent to a significant portion of a household mortgage or a luxury annual holiday.

The Cost of Disposable Vapes in Australia

A high-quality disposable vape in Australia (such as the IGET Bar 3500 or Alibarbar Ingot 9000) costs between $33.00 and $55.00.

To compare the usage fairly, we need to look at puff equivalency:

  • A single tobacco cigarette provides roughly 10 to 15 puffs.
  • A pack of 20 cigarettes equals approximately 200 to 300 puffs.
  • An IGET Bar 3500 offers up to 3,500 puffs, which is the equivalent of roughly 10 to 12 packs of cigarettes.

Let's look at the average cost for vapers using equivalent nicotine strengths:

  • Casual Vaper (1 IGET Bar every 2 weeks): Approximately $17.50 per week, or $910.00 per year.
  • Moderate Vaper (1 IGET Bar per week): Approximately $35.00 per week, or $1,820.00 per year.
  • Heavy Vaper (2 IGET Bars per week): Approximately $70.00 per week, or $3,640.00 per year.

Tips to Optimise Vaping Savings

To get the absolute best value out of your vaping budget:

  1. Buy Wholesale Boxes: Purchasing vapes in wholesale boxes of 10 (such as a Waka SoPro Box or HQD Cuvie Box) reduces the cost per unit significantly, dropping individual vape prices to as low as $19.50.
  2. Proper Device Storage: Keep your vapes stored at room temperature. Extreme cold depletes lithium batteries quickly, leaving you with unused e-liquid inside the device when the battery dies.
  3. Optimise Puff Length: Avoid drawing excessively long drags (over 5 seconds), which burns through coil cotton and e-liquid faster than necessary.
